Brenda Holloway's Career

Brenda Holloway is a singer and songwriter of soul music. She is known for her songs such as: "Every Little Bit Hurts", "When I'm Gone", and "You've Made Me So Very Happy". Brenda was active in her career from 1962 until the 1990s where she mostly retired from the music industry. Brenda's last album, "My Love is Your Love" was released in 2003.

Holloway's Time with Motown Records

Brenda Holloway, after signing with Motown's Tamla division, had the choice to either relocate to Detroit for recording or stay in Los Angeles, where Motown was expanding its West Coast operations. She chose to remain in Los Angeles initially, and her early Motown records were produced there. She later toured with the Supremes and released several more singles, including "When I'm Gone." Despite some success, Holloway faced challenges with Motown, including creative differences and feeling disregarded. She eventually left Motown during a recording session with Smokey Robinson, returning to Los Angeles. Brenda Holloway's career continued to have its ups and downs, including legal battles, but her impact on the music industry remains notable.
